Last updated: April 2026  ·  QUID Free Prize Draw

No purchase necessary. A free postal entry route exists and carries equal odds to paid entries. See Section 5 for details.

1. Eligibility

  • Open to UK residents aged 18 years or older only.
  • Employees of QUID, their immediate families, and associated third parties are excluded.
  • By entering, you confirm you meet the eligibility criteria.
  • QUID reserves the right to verify eligibility and disqualify any entry that does not comply.

2. Weekly Draw Mechanics

  • Draws take place every Monday at 8:00pm GMT.
  • Each valid entry received by Sunday 23:59 GMT is included in that week's draw.
  • The prize pot equals the total number of valid entries that week multiplied by £1.
  • There is no rollover — the full pot is awarded every week without exception.
  • If no valid entries are received in a given week, no draw takes place and no prize is awarded.

3. Random Winner Selection

  • The winner is selected using a cryptographically secure random number generator applied to the pool of all valid entries for that week.
  • The selection process is conducted by an independent automated system and is not subject to human influence.
  • Each valid entry has exactly one chance per week regardless of the method of entry.
  • The draw result is final and binding. No correspondence will be entered into regarding the outcome.
  • The winner will be notified via the email address provided at time of entry within 24 hours of the draw.

4. Paid Entry

  • Paid entry costs £1 per week, billed monthly at £4.
  • Payment is processed securely. No payment data is stored by QUID.
  • Subscriptions can be cancelled at any time before the next billing cycle via your account settings.
  • Refunds are not available for completed billing periods.

5. Free Postal Entry Route

To enter for free by post: Write your full name, date of birth, email address, and the words "QUID ENTRY" on a plain piece of paper and send it to:

QUID Free Entry, PO Box 12345, London, EC1A 1BB

One postal entry per envelope. Entries must be received by Sunday 23:59 GMT to be included in that week's draw.
  • Free postal entries are entered into the same draw pool as paid entries.
  • Free and paid entries carry identical odds of winning.
  • No minimum number of postal entries is required for the draw to proceed.
  • Bulk or photocopied entries will be disqualified.
  • QUID accepts no responsibility for entries lost, delayed, or damaged in the post.

6. Equal Odds Guarantee

  • Every valid entry — whether paid or free postal — is assigned a single unique token in the draw pool.
  • The random selection draws from this unified pool with no weighting applied to any entry.
  • Paying more does not increase your odds. One entry = one chance, regardless of method.

7. Prize Distribution

  • The winner receives 90% of the weekly pot. A 10% platform fee is retained by QUID to cover operating costs.
  • Prize is transferred via bank transfer to the winner's nominated UK bank account within 5 working days of the draw.
  • Winners must provide valid UK bank details to claim their prize within 14 days of notification, or the prize will be forfeited.
  • Prizes cannot be exchanged for any other form of payment or transferred to a third party.
  • QUID reserves the right to publicise the winner's username (not full name) for promotional purposes unless the winner opts out in writing.

8. Anti-Fraud Rules

  • Multiple accounts, automated entries, or any attempt to manipulate the draw will result in immediate disqualification and a permanent ban.
  • QUID reserves the right to withhold prizes pending fraud investigation and to take legal action where appropriate.
  • Any entry submitted using false identity information will be void.
  • QUID may require winners to provide proof of identity before releasing a prize.
  • Suspicious activity will be reported to relevant authorities.

9. Governing Law

These Terms are governed by the laws of England and Wales. Any disputes shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the English courts. QUID operates as a free prize draw and is not subject to the Gambling Act 2005.
